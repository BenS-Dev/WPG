Origins & Expressions of Money

Explore the beginnings of currency and the colorful phrases we still use today.

💵 Currency and History

  • First Paper Money: First used in China during the Tang Dynasty (618–907 AD).
  • Ancient Coins: First metal coins appeared around 600 BCE in Lydia (modern Turkey).
  • Largest Bill: U.S. $100,000 note (1934), only for inter-bank use.
  • “$” Symbol: Likely evolved from overlapping Spanish peso “P” and “S.”
  • First ATM: Installed in London in 1967; inventor John Shepherd-Barron.

🏛 Historical Tidbits

  • Wampum: Native Americans used shell beads as money.
  • Tulip Mania: 1637 Dutch tulip-bulb bubble—the first recorded financial bubble.
  • Salt Wages: Roman soldiers paid in salt (“worth his salt”).
  • Europe’s First Banknotes: Sweden, 1661.

📜 Money Phrases & Expressions

  • “Buck” Origin: Trading deer skins (“bucks”).
  • “Cold Hard Cash”: Refers to the metallic coldness of coins.
  • “Money Doesn’t Grow on Trees”: Emphasizing scarcity.

Material & Security of Money

Delve into what money is made of and the security features that protect it.

💳 Money Material

  • U.S. Bills: 75% cotton, 25% linen—no wood pulp paper.
  • Polymer Notes: Australia introduced plastic bills in 1988.
  • Circulation Lifespan: Coins ∼30 years; bills ∼18 months.

🔍 Hidden Features

  • Secret Imagery: Tiny owl or spider on the U.S. $1 bill.
  • Security Elements: Watermarks, holograms, UV-reactive inks.
  • Microprinting: Extremely small text to foil counterfeiters.
  • Intaglio Printing: Raised ink you can feel by touch.

🪙 Coins and Quirks

  • Coin Toss Bias: Lands on its starting side about 51% of the time.
  • Penny Cost: Costs ∼2 ¢ to mint a 1 ¢ coin.
  • Numismatics: The study and collection of coins.
  • Largest Coin: Australia’s 2011 pure-gold, 1-ton legal-tender coin.
  • Mint Marks: Small letters identifying the mint of origin.

Global Currency Systems

Discover how money operates worldwide and the institutions that safeguard it.

🌍 Global Money Facts

  • Most Traded: U.S. dollar involved in ≈88% of forex trades.
  • Weakest Currencies: Iranian Rial & Venezuelan Bolívar.
  • Strongest Currency: Kuwaiti Dinar.
  • Euro Launch: Notes & coins introduced January 1, 2002.
  • Zimbabwe Hyperinflation: $100 trillion note issued 2008.

🏦 Banks & Vaults

  • Largest Gold Reserve: Federal Reserve Bank of New York.
  • Most Secure Vault: Fort Knox, USA.
  • Oldest Bank: Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena (est. 1472).
  • First Credit Card: Diners Club, 1950.

🧮 Economic Oddities

  • Pennies Accumulate: Lost or discarded pennies total millions each year.

Psychology & Trivia of Money

Uncover the quirks of how we think about money and some fun trivia.

💰 Money Psychology

  • Happiness Threshold: Well-being rises with income up to ≈$75 000/year.
  • Spending Habits: People spend less when using cash vs. card.
  • Chrometophobia: Irrational fear of money.
  • New Money Smell: Unique scent from cotton-linen blend.

💡 Money Trivia

  • Piggy Bank: Evolved from “pygg” clay jars into the pig shape.
  • Richest Individual: John D. Rockefeller (inflation-adjusted).
  • Monopoly Money: More printed annually than many real currencies.
  • Forex Market: Largest financial market—∼$7 trillion/day.

📉 Money Mistakes & Mishaps

  • Misprints: Some errors fetch thousands of times face value.
  • Bank Errors: Occasional accidental large deposits into wrong accounts.

Rare & Space-Bound Currency

From the most expensive coins to currency beyond Earth’s bounds.

🌌 Money in Space

  • Space Coins: Astronauts carry commemorative coins into orbit.
  • Space Currency: Proposals exist for off-world monetary systems.

🤑 High-Dollar Items

  • 1933 Double Eagle: U.S. gold coin sold for over $18 million.
  • Bitcoin’s First Price: $0.0008 in 2010, now worth thousands.
